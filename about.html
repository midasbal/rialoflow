What Is RialoFlow?

RialoFlow is a tiny interactive storyboard about autonomous finance – inspired by Rialo’s idea of a self‑driving treasury.

There is no real money, no blockchain, no smart contracts here. It’s just a simulation that helps you see how a treasury could manage itself with rules and scheduled actions.

What You Can Do

On the main page you can:

  • See the portfolio: A simple treasury split into USDC, T‑Bills, bonds, and cash, plus total value and a basic “reserve ratio”. You’ll also see a simple Risk Score (0–100) that moves with your settings. It’s not real risk modeling – just a toy signal to show how an autonomous treasury could “feel” market stress.
  • Tweak the rules: Sliders let you change market shock size, target reserve ratio, and yield distribution percentage. If you don’t want to think too hard, use the scenario presets (Conservative, Balanced, Aggressive) to instantly load different treasury personalities and rerun the story.
  • Play the story: A short timeline shows four moments:
    • T+0 – Initial state
    • T+1 – Market shock
    • T+2 – Auto‑rebalance
    • T+3 – Scheduled yield payout

    • Each step also shows how the total value changed from the previous one, so you can see the impact of shocks, rebalances, and payouts in actual numbers.

    A small Simulation Log at the bottom writes out what happened at each step – like a mini trace of the treasury’s decisions.

Hit Play and watch how the portfolio and rules react step by step.

Why This Exists

Today, this kind of behavior usually needs off‑chain bots, cron jobs, keeper networks, and bundled transactions.

Rialo’s vision is closer to:

  • On‑chain programs with native scheduling and real‑world data
  • Fewer bots, fewer scripts, more logic inside the protocol

RialoFlow doesn’t run on Rialo. It’s an educational toy: a simple way to explore what a self‑driving treasury on something like Rialo could feel like.

The Risk Score, presets, and all the math here are deliberately simplified. They’re meant to explain ideas, not to act as financial advice or real‑world models.

Who It’s For

  • DeFi / crypto people curious about “autonomous finance”
  • Builders and designers thinking about treasuries, risk, and scheduling
  • Anyone who just wants to move sliders, hit Play, and see what happens
